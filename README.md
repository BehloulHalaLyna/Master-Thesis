# Master Thesis — AI Integration in Agile Project Management

This repository contains the materials related to my Master’s thesis in Information Systems (MIAGE) at Université Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne.

## Title

**How is Artificial Intelligence Integrated into Agile Project Management?**  
*A Mixed-Method Study of French Agile Teams*

## Overview

This thesis explores how Artificial Intelligence is integrated into Agile project management practices in French organizations. It focuses on the AI tools used by Agile teams, the Agile phases where AI is applied, and the perceived benefits and challenges of AI adoption.

The study uses a mixed-method approach combining:

- A survey of **100 Agile practitioners**
- **4 semi-structured interviews** with Agile, management, and technical profiles

## Research Focus

The thesis investigates:

- The types of AI tools used in Agile project teams
- AI integration across Agile phases: Envision, Speculate, Explore, Adapt, and Close
- The impact of AI on team performance, deliverable quality, and project success
- Challenges such as GDPR compliance, resistance to change, documentation burden, and collaboration shifts

## Key Findings

AI adoption in Agile teams is real but uneven. Generative AI and code assistants are mainly used in development, testing, backlog management, and documentation tasks.

The study identifies three main organizational tensions:

1. **Documentation paradox** — AI accelerates documentation but requires more detailed specifications.
2. **V-cycle gravity effect** — AI-assisted workflows may encourage more sequential work patterns.
3. **Collaboration reduction** — Teams may rely more on AI tools and less on informal knowledge-sharing.
